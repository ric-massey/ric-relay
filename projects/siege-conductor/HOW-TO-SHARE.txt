SIEGE CONDUCTOR — how to put it online and send it to friends
==============================================================

You can't text the app itself — you put it online once (free, ~2 min),
get a LINK, and text the link. Friends tap the link and add it to their
home screen as an app. No App Store, no warnings, nothing gets blocked.


STEP 1 — Put it online (do this once)
-------------------------------------
Easiest option: Netlify Drop (free, no setup)

  1. Go to:  https://app.netlify.com/drop
  2. Drag the WHOLE "siege-conductor" folder onto the page.
     (the folder this file is in — not just one file)
  3. Wait a few seconds. It gives you a link like:
        https://something-random-1234.netlify.app
  4. (Optional) Make a free account when it asks, so the link stays
     forever and you can rename it to something nicer.

That link IS the app. Open it on any phone or computer.

Other free drag-and-drop hosts that work the same way:
  - https://tiiny.host
  - https://surge.sh  (a bit more technical)


STEP 2 — Send it
----------------
Paste the link into Messages / WhatsApp / email — anything.
It's just a normal web link, so nothing blocks it.


STEP 3 — What your friend does (tell them this)
-----------------------------------------------
On iPhone:
  1. Tap the link (it opens in Safari).
  2. Tap the Share button (square with an arrow).
  3. Tap "Add to Home Screen."
  4. Tap "Add." Done — it's now an app icon (the crossed sabers).

On Android:
  1. Tap the link (opens in Chrome).
  2. Tap the ⋮ menu, then "Add to Home screen" / "Install app."

On Mac/Windows:
  - Just open the link in any browser. (Chrome/Edge also show an
    "install" icon in the address bar if they want an app window.)

After the first open it works even with no internet, and it remembers
which step you're on.


Notes
-----
- iPhone uses the "apple-touch-icon.png" for the home-screen icon.
- The "icon-1024.png" file isn't required for the web app; it's just a
  big master copy in case you ever want it.
- To update the app later: change the files, then re-drag the folder to
  the same host (Netlify lets you redeploy over the same site).
