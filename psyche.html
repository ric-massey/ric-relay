Psyche Human systems, observed from inside

Patterns, criteria, chemistry, and the evidence we use to understand ourselves—assembled by one of the systems being studied.

Boundary note: these are personal models, published criteria, and evidence frameworks. They are invitations to think, not diagnoses or medical advice.

Instrument 01 / signal over time

An animated comparison of bipolar mood episodes and a typical baseline. It turns a calendar of changing internal weather into a moving signal.

The Shape of Harm

An evidence-informed framework for comparing psychoactive-substance harms while keeping uncertainty, source quality, and methodological limits visible.

The connecting thread: these projects do not claim that autism, bipolar disorder, and substance harm are the same subject. They examine the same difficult act from different angles: translating complex human experience into signals, categories, and evidence without confusing the model for the person.
